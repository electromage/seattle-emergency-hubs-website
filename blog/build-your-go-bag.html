Build Your 72-Hour Go Bag

February 15, 2026 · Preparedness Committee

A 72-hour go bag (also called a bug-out bag or emergency kit) is a pre-packed bag that contains everything you need to survive for three days if you have to leave your home quickly or if services are disrupted after a disaster.

Why 72 Hours?

Emergency managers plan for a scenario where formal assistance (police, fire, utilities) may not reach everyone for up to 72 hours after a major disaster. Having three days of supplies gives you the buffer to be self-sufficient while the broader response gets organized.

Essential Items

Water

  • 1 gallon per person per day (3 gallons per person minimum)
  • Water purification tablets or a filter straw as backup

Food

  • Non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items
  • Energy bars, canned goods, dried fruit, nuts
  • Manual can opener
  • Don't forget special dietary needs and foods for children or pets

First Aid

  • Basic first aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, gauze, tape)
  • Prescription medications (minimum 7-day supply)
  • Copies of important medical documents

Documents & Cash

  • Copies of IDs, passports, insurance cards
  • Cash in small bills (ATMs may be down)
  • List of important phone numbers (don't rely on your phone's battery)

Tools & Safety

  • Flashlight and extra batteries (or a hand-crank light)
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Multi-tool or Swiss army knife
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Dust masks or N95 respirators
  • Duct tape and plastic sheeting
  • Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities

Warmth & Shelter

  • Emergency Mylar blankets (one per person)
  • Rain poncho
  • Extra set of warm clothes and sturdy shoes
  • Sleeping bag if space allows

Communication

  • Portable phone charger / power bank (kept charged)
  • List of family meeting points if you're separated

Maintenance

Review your go bag every six months. Replace expired food and medication, rotate water, and update documents. We recommend checking on daylight saving time changes as a reminder.
