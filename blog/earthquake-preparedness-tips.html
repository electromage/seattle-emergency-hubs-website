Earthquake Preparedness Tips for Seattle Residents

January 5, 2026 · Preparedness Committee

Seattle sits atop or near several active seismic zones, including the Cascadia Subduction Zone, the Seattle Fault, and the South Whidbey Island Fault. A major earthquake in this region could cause widespread disruption for days or weeks. Here's what every household should do to prepare.

1. Secure Your Space

  • Strap water heaters and large appliances to walls
  • Anchor bookshelves, cabinets, and tall furniture
  • Store heavy items on lower shelves
  • Know where your gas shutoff valve is and keep a wrench nearby
  • Identify safe spots in each room: under sturdy tables, against interior walls away from windows

2. Build Your Kit

Aim for a minimum of two weeks of supplies for a major earthquake scenario:

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day
  • Food: Non-perishable items for 2 weeks
  • Medications: At least a 2-week supply
  • First aid kit, flashlights, batteries, and a radio
  • Cash in small bills
  • Copies of important documents in a waterproof bag

3. Make a Communication Plan

  • Choose an out-of-area contact that all family members will check in with
  • Designate a meeting spot if you can't get home
  • Know your children's school and employer's emergency plans
  • Learn about your neighborhood emergency hub — it could be your first point of contact after a quake

4. Practice Drop, Cover, and Hold On

During shaking: Drop to hands and knees, Cover your head and neck under a sturdy table or against an interior wall, and Hold On until the shaking stops. Do not run outside during shaking.

5. Know Your Risk

Check if your home is in a liquefaction zone or landslide hazard area using Seattle's OEM hazard maps. Older, unreinforced masonry buildings have the highest risk of collapse. If you're a renter, ask your landlord about the building's seismic retrofit status.
