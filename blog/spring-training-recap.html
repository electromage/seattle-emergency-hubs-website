Spring Training Day Recap

January 20, 2026 · Training Team

What a day! Over 60 volunteers from hubs across Seattle gathered at Rainier Community Center for our first large-scale training event of the year. The energy was fantastic, and everyone came away with new skills and stronger connections.

Morning Sessions

The morning kicked off with a CERT refresher course led by Seattle Fire Department instructors. Topics included updated triage protocols, fire suppression basics, and a hands-on light search-and-rescue drill in the parking lot.

We also held a first-aid skills station where volunteers practiced CPR, wound care, and splinting — with a special session on pediatric first aid.

Afternoon Workshops

After lunch, we split into smaller groups for three concurrent workshops:

  • Emergency Communications: An introduction to GMRS radios and how to use them during a disaster when cell networks are down.
  • Hub Logistics: How to set up, run, and demobilize a hub activation — including supply management and volunteer coordination.
  • Community Outreach: Strategies for reaching neighbors who haven't yet heard of emergency hubs, with a focus on multilingual outreach.

What's Next

Our next large training event is scheduled for May. In the meantime, individual hubs are holding their own monthly meetings — check the calendar for dates near you.

If you missed this event, don't worry — we're working on a recording of the morning session. Sign up for our mailing list to be notified when it's available.
